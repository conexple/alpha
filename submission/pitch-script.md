# Pitch video script — ≤ 3 minutes

> Audience: Colosseum judges (investors evaluating for the
> accelerator). They are NOT primarily technical reviewers — see
> `instruction/work/colosseum-intel.md §Judging weight`.
>
> Goal of THIS video: convince them this team will build a real
> startup, not ship a hackathon and disappear. Tech goes in the
> separate technical demo video.

## Visual structure

- Slide-style frames (use the deck — see `pitch-deck-outline.md`)
- Founder on camera (face cam) for hook + close
- Voice-over slides for the middle

## Storyboard (target run-time 2:50)

### 0:00–0:20 — Hook + identity (face cam)

**Bosso, on camera, in front of a Bangkok backdrop or branded slide.**

> "I'm Bosso, building Conexple in Thailand.
>
> Right now, when you buy something online, the merchant pays an
> affiliate commission — and that money goes to an influencer. Almost
> never to you, the person who actually bought it.
>
> Conexple redirects that pool — the merchant's existing affiliate
> budget — to the people who actually purchased. On-chain. On Solana."

### 0:20–0:50 — The market (slide: Thailand $10B + ASEAN map)

**Slide: Thailand commerce map + numbers.**

> "Thailand alone moves $10 billion a year through e-commerce
> affiliate programs. Across South-East Asia it's roughly $40 billion.
>
> The buyer never sees a cut.
>
> We're a Thai team. We know this market. Our company, Sornkan Co.,
> already operates in Thai consumer brands. Conexple is the protocol
> we wanted to use ourselves and didn't find."

### 0:50–1:30 — How it works (one diagram)

**Slide: the ÷7 split visualization.**

> "Every purchase splits into seven parts.
>
> Five go to the buyer's upline — five levels deep. One funds a
> public social pool, on-chain. The seventh is an Infinity Override
> for long-term active members.
>
> The merchant pays nothing extra. They were already paying this
> commission to influencers. We're just redistributing it.
>
> And every rule — every commission, every payout, every position —
> is on Solana, public, verifiable. We can't change the rules without
> everyone seeing it."

### 1:30–2:00 — Why this isn't MLM (slide: the table)

**Slide: anti-MLM contrast table.**

> "We've heard the obvious objection. So we designed this so it
> structurally can't be MLM.
>
> 50% margin cap, enforced on-chain. So real product value can't fall
> below half the price.
>
> No recruitment requirement. Your earnings depend on purchases, not
> on people you signed up.
>
> No auto-assign. No genesis position. No pyramid economics.
>
> If the operator misbehaves, the protocol — open source, Apache 2.0 —
> can be forked. We don't own the network."

### 2:00–2:30 — Demo flash + Solana fit (slide: solscan tx)

**Cut to: 5-second screen flash of the deployed demo with one Solscan
link visible. Then back to slide.**

> "Here's the live demo on Solana devnet — populated network, one
> purchase, settlement transaction on Solscan.
>
> Why Solana? Because per-purchase commissions at sub-cent fees only
> make sense here. Daily settlement runs at zero gas. USDC settlement
> matches what merchants already understand.
>
> Helius and Triton are partners — we're already on their devnet."

### 2:30–2:50 — Ask + close (face cam)

> "We're submitting Conexple to Frontier as a hackathon prototype.
>
> Post-hackathon, we're operating Conexple Thailand as the first
> network. We'll onboard 5 merchants in Q3, scale to 50 by year-end.
>
> The protocol stays open. Any operator in any country can deploy it.
> That's how we go from $10 billion in Thailand to the rest of
> Southeast Asia.
>
> The repo, the demo, the deck — all linked. Talk soon."

## Tips for recording

- Record audio separately if you can — phone voice memo > laptop mic.
- Prefer 1080p screen recording over 720p.
- One take per section is fine; you'll cut on the slide transitions.
- Don't apologize on camera. If you stumble, restart that section.
- If you don't have great B-roll, white slides with one number on
  them is *more* trustworthy than over-produced graphics.
