# Pitch video script — ≤ 3 minutes

> Audience: Colosseum judges (investors evaluating for the
> accelerator). They are NOT primarily technical reviewers — see
> `instruction/work/colosseum-intel.md §Judging weight`.
>
> Goal of THIS video: convince them this team will build a real
> startup, not ship a hackathon and disappear. Tech goes in the
> separate technical demo video.

## Visual structure

- Slide-style frames (use the deck — see `pitch-deck-outline.md`)
- Founder on camera (face cam) for hook + close
- Voice-over slides for the middle

## Storyboard (target run-time 2:45)

### 0:00–0:20 — Hook + identity (face cam)

**Bosso, on camera, in front of a Bangkok backdrop or branded slide.**

> "I'm Bosso, building Conexple in Thailand.
>
> When you buy something online, the merchant pays an affiliate
> commission — and that money goes to an influencer. Almost never to
> you, the person who actually bought it.
>
> Conexple redirects that pool — the merchant's existing affiliate
> budget — to the people who actually purchased. On-chain. On Solana."

### 0:20–0:45 — The market (slide: Thailand $10B + ASEAN map)

**Slide: Thailand commerce map + numbers.**

> "Thailand alone moves around ten billion dollars a year through
> e-commerce affiliate programs. Across South-East Asia it's roughly
> forty billion.
>
> The buyer never sees a cut.
>
> We're a Thai team. Our company, Sornkan Co., already operates in
> Thai consumer brands. Conexple is the protocol we wanted to use
> ourselves and didn't find."

### 0:45–1:25 — How it works (one diagram)

**Slide: the ÷7 split visualization.**

> "Every purchase splits into seven parts.
>
> Five go to the buyer's upline — five levels deep. One funds a
> public social pool. The seventh is an Infinity Override for
> long-term active members.
>
> The merchant pays nothing extra. They were already paying this
> commission to influencers. We just redistribute it.
>
> Every rule — every commission, every payout, every position — is
> on Solana, public, verifiable. We can't change the rules without
> everyone seeing it."

### 1:25–1:55 — Why this isn't MLM (slide: the table)

**Slide: anti-MLM contrast table.**

> "We've heard the objection, so we designed this so it structurally
> can't be MLM.
>
> Fifty-percent margin cap, enforced on-chain. Real product value
> can't fall below half the price.
>
> No recruitment requirement. Earnings depend on purchases, not on
> people you signed up.
>
> No auto-assign. No genesis position. No pyramid economics.
>
> The protocol is Apache 2.0. If the operator misbehaves, anyone
> can fork it. We don't own the network."

### 1:55–2:25 — Demo flash + Solana fit (slide: solscan tx)

**Cut to: 5-second screen flash of the deployed demo with one
Solscan link visible. Then back to slide.**

> "Here's the live demo on Solana devnet. Four Anchor programs,
> twenty-one positions across three trees, four merchants — including
> one third-party merchant that plugged in end-to-end on chain.
>
> Why Solana? Per-purchase commissions at sub-cent fees only make
> sense here. Daily settlement runs at near-zero gas. USDC matches
> what merchants already understand.
>
> Helius and Triton are partners — we're on their devnet."

### 2:25–2:45 — Ask + close (face cam)

> "We're submitting Conexple to Frontier as a hackathon prototype,
> opting into the Public Goods award. Apache 2.0, no operator token.
>
> Post-hackathon, we operate Conexple Thailand as the first network.
> Five merchants in Q3, fifty by year-end.
>
> The protocol stays open. Any operator in any country can deploy it.
> That's how we go from ten billion in Thailand to the rest of ASEAN.
>
> Repo, demo, and deck — all linked. Talk soon."

## Tips for recording

- Record audio separately if you can — phone voice memo > laptop mic.
- Prefer 1080p screen recording over 720p.
- One take per section is fine; you'll cut on the slide transitions.
- Don't apologize on camera. If you stumble, restart that section.
- If you don't have great B-roll, white slides with one number on
  them is *more* trustworthy than over-produced graphics.
